# AR Card Deck Builder - Testing Consent And Release Form

Project: **AR Card Deck Builder**  
Developer: **Peter**  
Project type: **Unity / Vuforia AR card deck builder**

## Purpose Of Testing

You are being asked to test a student development project called **AR Card Deck Builder**. The project allows users to build a custom card deck in Unity, scan printed card images using a camera, and display interactive 3D models, stats, sound effects, and particle animations through augmented reality.

The purpose of this testing is to collect feedback on how understandable, usable, and enjoyable the project is. Your feedback may be used to improve the project and may be discussed in the developer's project journal, report, or final evaluation.

## What You Will Be Asked To Do

During the test session, you may be asked to:

- Try the deck builder workflow in Unity.
- Add or edit cards in a deck.
- Load or reload runtime image targets.
- Scan printed cards using the camera setup.
- Observe 3D models, stats, sound effects, and particle animations.
- Interact with the 3D model and test spell/rule card behavior where available.
- Give verbal or written feedback about what worked, what was confusing, and what could be improved.

Testing should take around **15-30 minutes**, unless you choose to spend longer.

## Voluntary Participation

Taking part in this testing is voluntary. You can stop testing at any time without needing to give a reason. If you feel uncomfortable, confused, or do not want to continue, you can ask to stop.

You do not need to answer any question you do not want to answer.

## Data And Privacy

The project does not require sensitive personal information. The developer may record basic testing notes, such as:

- General feedback you give about the project.
- Observations about whether features were easy or difficult to use.
- Suggestions you make for improving the deck builder, AR scanning, visuals, sound, or interaction.
- General comments about bugs, errors, or usability problems.

Your name will not be used in the project journal, report, or presentation unless you give permission. Feedback will normally be described anonymously, for example as "a Unity-focused tester" or "a trading card game player".

Any testing notes will be stored only for project development and assessment purposes. They will not be sold, shared publicly as personal data, or used for unrelated purposes.

## Possible Risks Or Discomforts

This is a low-risk student project test. However:

- The project uses a camera to scan printed cards.
- The project may include sound effects.
- The project may include bright particle effects, fire effects, or flashing movement.
- Unity or Vuforia may behave unexpectedly during testing.

If any sound, visual effect, or camera use makes you uncomfortable, you can ask for it to be stopped.
